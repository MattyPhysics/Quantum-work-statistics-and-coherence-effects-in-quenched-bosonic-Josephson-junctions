# Abstract
We investigate the non-equilibrium work statistics originated by a sudden quench in a bosonic Josephson junction. In particular, by employing the Holstein-Primakoff approximation, the work statistics are analytically characterized in the weak-interaction regime, mapping the dynamics to a time-dependent quantum harmonic oscillator. For a junction initialized in the ground state of the undriven Hamiltonian, we demonstrate that the work statistics are governed by a negative binomial distribution, as it occurs for fully-connected models crossing a critical point. Furthermore, we also consider initial superposition states that include quantum coherence terms in relation to the energy basis. To characterize the work distribution in this case, we resort to Kirkwood-Dirac quasiprobabilities (KDQ). Even in the simpler case the junction state is the superposition of the ground and first excited states, the KDQ distribution of work exhibits negative or complex values. Moreover, the coherence content of the initial state can be optimized in order to enhance the extractable work due to the quench, beyond classical bounds. Finally, we propose an experimental interferometric protocol to directly measure the characteristic function of the work distribution in real settings.
